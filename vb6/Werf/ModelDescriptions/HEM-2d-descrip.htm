General Description

HEM-2d is a laterally-averaged two-dimensional intra-tidal model, suitable for application to narrow, elongated tidal rivers and estuaries. It can handle tributaries (Hsu et al. 1999), and treats the shallow shoals or embayments by the side of main channel kinematically as side storage areas (Kuo and Park, 1995). The model is an integrated hydrodynamic and eutrophication model.The hydrodynamic and water quality portions of the model are internally linked, while their computations are decoupled (Park et al. 1998). This makes the revision of program code for kinetic processes much easier.

The hydrodynamic portion of the model may be run alone. The eutrophication portion of the model has to be run together with hydrodynamic model, though larger time step is permissible

Model methods include the following:

Finite difference method, z-coordinate in vertical direction.

 

Limitations

Limited to tidal river and estuaries without substantial lateral variation.
